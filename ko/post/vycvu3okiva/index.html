5 Years with the M1 MacBook Air: Still Worth It?

In this blog post, we delve into an updated review of the M1 MacBook Air, five years after its launch. This laptop has been celebrated for its performance, portability, and exceptional battery life. We’ll explore its design, performance, battery longevity, and current market position to determine if it still holds value in today’s MacBook lineup.

M1 MacBook Air Overview

  • Purchased 6 months after release, 5 years ago
  • Highly regarded for performance and portability
  • Current retail price: $650 new, $550 used

The M1 MacBook Air was bought six months post-launch and has consistently impressed with its blend of performance, portability, and price. Available currently from retailers like Walmart for $650 fresh out of the box or $550 used from places like Amazon, it’s still a solid choice.

Design Aesthetic

  • Sleek low taper fade design
  • Two USB-C ports and headphone jack
  • Upgraded keyboard avoids butterfly issues
  • 13.3-inch Retina display

The M1 MacBook Air features a stylish low taper fade design with practical ports and an upgraded keyboard that enhances user experience. Its 13.3-inch Retina display offers outstanding color clarity compared to newer models.

Performance Analysis

  • Exceptional laptop performance for $500-$700
  • Strong battery life despite age
  • Handles everyday and creative tasks well

For the price bracket of around $500 to $700, the M1 MacBook Air delivers impressive performance, excelling in both creative and student applications, backed by a remarkably decent battery life.

Battery Life Insight

  • Claimed 18-hour battery life for new models
  • Real-world usage results in about 10 hours
  • Good battery life for intensive applications

Apple rates the M1 MacBook Air for 18 hours of video playback, but real-world usage reveals approximately 10 hours of productivity. Even with heavy applications, it still performs admirably, offering significant battery longevity.

RAM and Storage Limitations

  • Base model constrained to 8GB RAM
  • Unified memory efficiency
  • Upgrades to 16GB RAM recommended for heavy tasks

The base M1 MacBook Air offers only 8GB RAM, which, while efficient due to Apple’s Unified Memory architecture, may require upgrades for memory-intensive tasks or applications. Current offerings suggest a significant advantage to variants with 16GB of RAM.

Storage Needs

  • Options for 256GB or more storage
  • External SSD as a solution
  • Importance of storage size based on use case

With a 256GB storage capacity, many users find it sufficient for basic tasks, though power users may need more. It’s wise to consider an external SSD for additional storage, especially for intensive content creation.

Software Support Longevity

  • Expected support for 7-8 years of macOS updates
  • Doubts about future app compatibility for students

The M1 MacBook Air is expected to receive macOS updates for approximately 7-8 years, making it a stable choice for students and professionals alike.

Depreciation and Durability

  • Strong resale value due to demand
  • Durability from quality materials

The M1 MacBook Air maintains a solid resale value owing to strong demand, and its durable aluminum design contributes to lasting performance. However, users should protect the display to avoid costly repairs.

Comparison with Newer Models

  • M2 and M3 bring upgrades that might attract buyers
  • M1 still viable for most users

While the newer M2 and M3 models offer exciting advancements, the M1 MacBook Air remains a relevant choice for many users who don’t require the latest specifications.
